Have not you yourselves sensed a difference in the light that suffuses such a room, a rare tranquility not found in ordinary light. . . . .

Have you never felt a sort of fear in the face of the ageless, a fear that in that room you might lose all consciousness of the passage of time. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

that untold years might pass and upon emerging you should find you had grown old and gray?

Live again
and cherish the shadow this time
